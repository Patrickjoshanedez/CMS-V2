# CHAPTER 5
## SUMMARY, CONCLUSIONS, AND RECOMMENDATIONS

## 5.1 Summary of Findings

This study focused on the design and development of a web-based Capstone Management System with Plagiarism Checker for managing the capstone process from proposal stage to final manuscript submission. The system was developed to address common challenges in manual capstone monitoring, including delayed feedback, fragmented document tracking, and inconsistent similarity-checking workflows.

Based on the development and implementation results discussed in Chapter 4, the project delivered core modules for role-based access, chapter-based submissions, adviser and panel review workflows, and integrated plagiarism checking for pre-final and final submissions. The system also supported transparent status monitoring of student progress across the capstone lifecycle.

The results indicated that the developed platform improved the organization of capstone artifacts and streamlined communication among students, advisers, and panel members. Through centralized document handling and structured submission stages, the system reduced process ambiguity and supported a more consistent academic review flow.

## 5.2 Conclusions

From the findings of the study, the following conclusions were drawn:

1. The developed Capstone Management System is a viable solution for digitizing and standardizing chapter-based capstone workflows from Chapter 1 to Chapter 5.
2. The integration of plagiarism checking functionality strengthens academic integrity by enabling earlier detection of high similarity content before final acceptance.
3. The role-specific workflow (students, advisers, and panel members) improves accountability and traceability of comments, revisions, and submission history.
4. Centralized submission and review processes can reduce administrative delays and improve the overall efficiency of capstone project management.
5. The system aligns with the intended objective of providing a structured, transparent, and scalable platform for capstone coordination within the institution.

Overall, the study confirms that a dedicated capstone platform with plagiarism support can significantly enhance both management efficiency and the quality-control process of student research outputs.

## 5.3 Recommendations

To further improve the system and strengthen institutional adoption, the following recommendations are proposed:

1. Implement analytics dashboards for coordinators to monitor completion rates, revision cycles, and chapter-level bottlenecks.
2. Add rubric-based digital evaluation forms for advisement and panel review to standardize grading and feedback quality.
3. Enhance notification mechanisms (email and in-app alerts) for deadlines, review actions, and approval milestones.
4. Expand plagiarism reporting with deeper source-link traceability and downloadable comparison summaries.
5. Conduct periodic usability testing with students, advisers, and panel members to continuously refine user experience.
6. Integrate institutional archive and backup strategies to preserve accepted manuscripts and revision histories.
7. For future researchers, perform comparative studies with other capstone management approaches to measure long-term impact on completion time, feedback quality, and research output standards.

## 5.4 Final Statement

The project demonstrated that a focused, web-based capstone management platform can bridge operational gaps in manuscript handling, improve review transparency, and support academic integrity through integrated similarity checking. With continued enhancement and institutional deployment, the system can serve as a sustainable foundation for improving capstone governance and research quality.
